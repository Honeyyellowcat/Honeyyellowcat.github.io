    • Thrust Measurement:

  • - Based on the given thrust range of 10 ùùªN to 100 mN and a 0.25 m height thruster position, the flexures‚Äô spring constants must all fall between 0.700 N*m < k < 7.81E4 N*m for an 8 kg thruster with a rangefinder precision of 10E-6 m.
  • - An 8 flexure design was selected.
  • - For the DawgStar‚Äôs PPT design (5 kg) with an average thrust of 5.68E-5 N [1] and moment arm of 0.25 m, this would correspond to k = 42.8 N*m.
  • - Ansys FEA was performed for a single leg of the pendulum due to software restrictions on the student version.
  • - An 8 flexure design was selected.
  • - Load of the thruster scaled to account for only simulating one leg of the pendulum.
  • - Total displacement of 0.0227 inches (0.576 mm) using 0.01 in thick spring steel flexure.
  • - The IL-100 rangefinder‚Äôs minimum resolution is 60 ùùªm.
  • - Requirement St.7 is met on the low end of the scale, flexure thickness can be changed to meet the upper end.
  • - Simple design using 4 bolts to hold flexures in place.
  • - Flexure bolts easily accessed when the test stand fully assembled, making for easy removal and installation.
  • - Flexures of many thicknesses ranging from 0.01 inches to 0.6 inches will be manufactured.
  • - Requirement St.8 will be met by utilizing this design strategy.

NovaTech Space Systems: Rocket Design

Summary

A group of venture capitalists expressed interest in funding a network of small satellites to provide satellite internet coverage globally. They tasked our team with designing a low-cost rocket featuring a reusable first stage capable of deploying a single 1,000 kg satellite into a 500 km altitude low-Earth orbit (LEO). Key design requirements included delivering the payload efficiently, minimizing costs, and ensuring reusability of the first stage.

NocaTech Rocket Prop Presentation

Fuel Choice

Staged Combustion Liquid Rocket Motors (LMR) using Oxygen (O2) and Hydrogen (H2) were chosen for both stages due to their cost-effectiveness and high specific impulse (Isp).

Turbomachinery

Turbopump masses and performance parameters were estimated for each stage to ensure efficient fuel delivery.

Combustion Chamber: Film Cooling

Film cooling techniques were employed to manage high combustion temperatures, with bell nozzle configurations adopted for optimal thrust.

Rocket Overview

Detailed analysis was conducted on the rocket's dimensions, stage burnout breakdown, and total cost projections, with a focus on meeting payload delivery requirements while optimizing cost efficiency.
